I'm Lizzy, and I'm a software engineer in Pittsburgh, PA. I graduated from Carnegie Mellon University in 2019 and am currently working for Maven Machines as a backend engineer. My current languages of choice are NodeJS and Python along with MySQL databases and Firebase. I'm also working on increasing my breadth of technology to become more full stack by learning frontend technologies like HTML, CSS, and React.

Bio

I grew up in Rochester, NY, as the daughter of two engineers enjoying the Kodak boom. Because of this, there were always two themes running through my life: computers and sports. As far back as I can remember, we always had a computer in the house that my sisters and I could play on, and when I wasn't on the computer, I was probably playing soccer or volleyball or dancing.

I graduated from Gates Chili High School in 2015 and decided to go to Carnegie Mellon as an electrical and computer engineering major (ECE) the following fall. While at CMU, I was also on the NCAA Varsity Volleyball team for all four years as a middle and was a TA for 15-112, the introductory programming class. I loved being part of a team in both aspects, where I had a group to depend on and who could, in turn, depend on me. I graduated from CMU in the spring of 2019 with a B.S. in ECE, an additional major in psychology, and a minor in computer science.

After graduating, I became a part of the Venture For America 2019 fellowship, a group that is focused on developing the next generation of startup entrepreneurs and leaders. I also rejoined Maven Machines after my internship the summer before to be a full-time backend engineer working on the dispatch project.
