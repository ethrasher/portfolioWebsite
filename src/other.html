Skiing

In January of 2021, I temporarily moved to Breckenridge, CO, to ski all winter. I was still working at Maven Machines in Pittsburgh, but since we were in the middle of the pandemic and working remotely anyway, I asked my boss if I could temporarily move.

I've been skiing since I was five and snowboarding since I was about 12 years old. I've skied tons of places in upstate NY, PA, and VT but had never been able to make a trip to ski out west.

It had always been a dream of mine to live next to a ski resort for a winter and be able to ski everyday throughout the winter. And that dream was finally realized when I could take advantage of working remotely.

Volleyball

I've played volleyball since I was 11, first starting at VolleyFX Volleyball Club and on my middle school modified team. I played every year through VFX 18s and high school varsity. I moved around positions a few times but was always a hitter in one form or another.

When I went to Carnegie Mellon, I decided to talk to the coach and tryout for the university's volleyball team as a walk-on. Carnegie Mellow is D3 and part of the UAA conference, so we traveled just about every weekend to play in tournaments. During our senior year, we made it to the Sweet 16 in the NCAA tournament

I can no longer play for a school, but I have found some leagues and pickup events around Pittsburgh. So, while I miss the team aspect a lot, I still get to enjoy the game I love.

Venture For America

I joined Venture For America as a 2019 fellow during my senior year of college.

Venture For America is a fellowship for new and recent grads focused on helping the next generation of entrepreneurs and startup leaders. It provides networking, mentorship, training, and generally support among peers.

After my internship at Maven Machines the year before, I really appreciated how, at a startup, I could wear a bunch of hats and have a lot of responsibility and ownership even as a recent grad engineer. While I'm still not sure how "entrepreneurial" I am, I do appreciate the leadership training and experience as well as the friends I've made through this fellowship.
